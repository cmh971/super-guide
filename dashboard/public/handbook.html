Welcome to the Team

Welcome aboard the California State Roleplay staff team. This handbook is your single source of truth.

As a member of staff you represent the community. Members will look to you for guidance, fairness, and consistency. Read this handbook fully and revisit it whenever you are unsure of a procedure.

Our Values

  • Fairness — every member is treated equally.
  • Transparency — actions are logged and explainable.
  • Professionalism — we stay calm under pressure.
  • Teamwork — we support each other and escalate when needed.

Code of Conduct

These rules apply to every staff member without exception.

  1. Treat every member and fellow staff member with respect at all times.
  2. No harassment, hate speech, discrimination, or targeted abuse — zero tolerance.
  3. Keep roleplay realistic; no fail-RP, RDM (random deathmatch), or VDM (vehicle deathmatch).
  4. Do not abuse staff permissions for personal gain or to favour friends.
  5. Always remain in character in IC channels and out of character in OOC channels.
  6. Escalate situations you are unsure about instead of guessing.
  7. Never share another member's personal information (doxxing) under any circumstance.
  8. Keep all moderation actions logged and justifiable with evidence.
  9. No advertising of other servers, services, or self-promotion without approval.
  10. Follow the chain of command for appeals and disputes.
  11. Maintain confidentiality of internal staff discussions.
  12. Do not engage with rule-breakers emotionally; stay professional and neutral.

Staff Ranks & Permissions

The chain of command from newest to most senior.

Trial Moderator

Warn, mute, kick. Handle low-priority tickets.

Moderator

All Trial perms plus temp-bans and ticket claiming.

Senior Moderator

Permanent bans, application review, mentoring trials.

Administrator

Server configuration, role management, escalations.

Head Administrator

Oversees the admin team and policy decisions.

Manager

Full operational control, hiring & firing of staff.

Standard Operating Procedures

Follow these step-by-step playbooks for common situations.

Handling a Support Ticket

  1. Claim the ticket so other staff know it is being handled.
  2. Greet the member politely and ask for details.
  3. Gather evidence (screenshots, IDs, timestamps).
  4. Resolve the issue or escalate to a higher rank if needed.
  5. Summarise the outcome and close the ticket; a transcript is saved automatically.

Reviewing a Staff Application

  1. Read the entire application before forming an opinion.
  2. Check the applicant's activity and history in the server.
  3. Discuss with at least one other staff member.
  4. Vote to approve or deny with a written reason.
  5. Notify the applicant of the decision respectfully.

Issuing a Moderation Action

  1. Confirm a rule was actually broken with evidence.
  2. Choose the lowest effective action (warn before mute, etc.).
  3. Apply the action and log it in the moderation channel.
  4. Inform the member which rule they broke.
  5. Watch for retaliation or evasion.

Running a Giveaway

  1. Confirm the prize and duration with an Administrator.
  2. Use the /giveaway command via the dashboard or in-server.
  3. Announce it in the events channel.
  4. Pick the winner fairly using the built-in roller.
  5. Deliver the prize and log it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common staff questions.

How do I claim a ticket?

Open the ticket channel and press the green Claim button at the top.

Can I ban without evidence?

No. Every action must be backed by evidence and logged.

What if a higher rank is breaking rules?

Report it privately to a Manager. Staff are not above the rules.

How do I use the dashboard?

Log in with Discord, pick your server from the DM, then run commands.

Why can't I run a command?

You may lack the staff role in the selected server, or it is not whitelisted.

How are transcripts stored?

Closed tickets are saved to MongoDB and DM'd to the opener.

Can the AI chat browse the web?

Yes — /chat now searches the web and cites its sources.

Who approves new staff?

Senior Moderators and above, after a team discussion.

What is fail-RP?

Acting unrealistically in a way that breaks immersion for others.

How do I appeal a decision?

Open a ticket and follow the chain of command.
